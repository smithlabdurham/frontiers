Term introduction
Assessment

This term will improve your skills in research and presentation by addressing current controversies in palaeontology. By engaging with active debates, you will learn to weigh evidence, to balance and present competing perspectives, and to acknowledge and represent uncertainty.

The term kicks off with two topical topics. After an introduction to the key concepts, we will engage in a class discussion ("Journal club") of an influential paper, then in groups you will have a week to prepare your case for a class debate in which your group must convince a judge of the rightness of your allocated side of the argument.

In the second part of the term, your group will focus on a more tightly defined research controversy. You will work to produce a microsite and brief oral primer that will together introduce your peers to the topic, and equip them to engage with the literature and reach their own conclusion about the issue.

This term is assessed by a two-hour final examination in which you will produce one essay on a topic addressed by one of the group presentations, and a second essay that may address either one of these group topics, or a 'topical topic' covered as a class in weeks 2–5. Essay questions will invite you to present an argument that draws on multiple aspects of the topic, including your own reading.
